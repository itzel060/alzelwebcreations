PASSIVE VOICE

The passive voice is employed to emphasize the person or object that undergoes an action, rather than the one performing it. Essentially, the key element or individual is made the subject of the sentence.

Causative verbs

Causative verbs are used to indicate that one person causes another person to do something on their behalf. This can be achieved by asking, paying, requesting, or compelling the person to perform the action.

Learn more...

Third conditional

The type 3 conditional describes an impossible condition in the past and its likely outcome in the past. These sentences are entirely hypothetical and unreal, as it is now too late for either the condition or its result to occur. Type 3 conditionals often imply regret. The reality is contrary to what the sentence conveys. In type 3 conditional sentences, the timeframe is the past, and the scenario is hypothetical.
