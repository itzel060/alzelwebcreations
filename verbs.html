CAUSATIVE VERBS

Causative verbs are used to indicate that one person causes another person to do something. The primary causative verbs in English are "make," "have," "get," and "let." Each of these verbs has a slightly different nuance and structure.

Structure of the Causative Verbs

  • Make:

    Structure: Subject + make + object + base verb

    The teacher made the students write an essay.

  • Have:

    Structure: Subject + have + object + base verb

    I had the mechanic fix my car.

  • Future Simple:

    The project will be completed by the team.

  • Present Continuous:

    The car is being fixed by the mechanic.

  • Get:

    Structure: Subject + get + object + to-infinitive

    She got her brother to help with the project.

  • Let

    Structure: Subject + let + object + base verb

    They let the children play outside.

Understanding causative verbs helps in expressing actions caused by others in a clear and concise manner. These verbs are essential for describing situations where someone is influencing another person to perform an action.
