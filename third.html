Passive Voice

THIRD CONDITIONAL

The third conditional is used to discuss hypothetical situations in the pastâ€”events that did not occur. It is commonly employed to convey regret or imagine alternate past outcomes.

Structure of the Third Conditional

If-clause: Utilizes the past perfect tense (had + past participle).

Main clause: Employs "would have" followed by the past participle of the verb.

If + past perfect, would have + past participle

Expressing Regret:

If I had studied harder, I would have passed the exam.

Imagining a Different Outcome:

If she had left earlier, she would have caught the train.

Describing Missed Opportunities:

If they had invested in that company, they would have made a fortune.

This structure incorporates the past perfect tense in the if-clause and "would have" plus the past participle in the main clause.
